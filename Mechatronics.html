Autonomous Mobile Robot for Maze Navigation and Air Hockey ("JHokey")

Johns Hopkins University

This project focused on the end-to-end design and implementation of an autonomous mobile robot capable of complex tasks such as maze traversal and competitive play in a custom air hockey–style game called JHokey.

  • Low-level PID control using encoder feedback for precise velocity regulation
  • High-level motion planning with a differential drive model, fusing IMU and camera data
  • Custom hardware platform with 3D-printed parts, sensors, and actuators
  • Arduino-based architecture supporting real-time I²C, UART, and GPIO communication

The robot demonstrated robust autonomous behavior, navigating mazes and strategically aiming and striking pucks to score during the JHokey competition.

Robot Design with integrated IR sensors, Pixy camera, IMU, Xbee sensor and a suction based puck grasper for the JHokey Tournament
