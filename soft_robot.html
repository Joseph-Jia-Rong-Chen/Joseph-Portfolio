Modularized Soft Robotics

Purdue University

This research project focused on the development of a modular soft robotic system designed for reconfigurability across multiple applications. The soft robot was designed with interchangeable segments, allowing different configurations to be assembled based on specific functional needs.

Using ABAQUS finite element simulation, I predicted the behavior of various robot configurations under actuation, enabling design validation prior to physical prototyping. Through iterative simulation and testing, I identified limitations in the initial design and improved the fabrication process by replacing the original suture-based assembly with a more reliable valve-based design, which enhanced both manufacturing efficiency and durability.
