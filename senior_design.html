Smart Memory Alloy Actuator

Senior Design · Purdue University

In an industry-sponsored project, our team was tasked with investigating recurring detachment issues reported with the B62-B5 fire hydrant, which have led to elevated maintenance costs. The objective was to identify the root cause of the failures and develop cost-effective design improvements that comply with relevant industry standards and operational requirements.

Valve improvements and manufacturing jig were designed and machined. Hydrostatic pressure and flow tests were conducted to ensure the functionality of the design.

The x, y, z vibration during fire hydrant operation was obtained using two accelerometers. The data was then converted into frequency domain using FFT before identifying the dominant resonant frequency that leads to the loosening of the valve.

Using a vibration tester, the effect of loosened valve was replicated

Using Ansys Fluent, the forces experienced by the valuve during operation was calculated
